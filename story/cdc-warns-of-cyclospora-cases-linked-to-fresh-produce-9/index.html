CDC Warns of Cyclospora Cases Linked to Fresh Produce

July 16, 2026

<div>Health officials are reminding consumers to wash fresh fruits and vegetables after an increase in reported Cyclospora infections in several areas.</div><div><br></div><div>Cyclospora is a microscopic parasite that can contaminate fresh produce such as leafy greens, herbs, berries, and other fruits and vegetables. Symptoms often include prolonged diarrhea, loss of appetite, stomach cramps, nausea, fatigue, and weight loss. Symptoms typically begin about a week after exposure and can last for several weeks if left untreated.</div><div><br></div><div>The CDC recommends thoroughly washing fresh produce, practicing good hand hygiene, and seeking medical attention if symptoms are severe or persist. While washing may help reduce some contamination, it may not completely eliminate the parasite.</div><div><br></div><div>Anyone experiencing prolonged diarrhea, especially after eating fresh produce or traveling, should contact their healthcare provider for evaluation and treatment.</div><div><br></div><div>Stay informed by following updates from your local health department and the CDC.</div>
