Tisha B'Av Reminder: A Guide for Adults & Children

July 22, 2026

<p>As we observe <strong>Tisha B'Av</strong>, we mourn the destruction of the <strong>Beis HaMikdash</strong> and reflect on how we can strengthen our relationship with Hashem and with one another.</p> <h3>Adults</h3> <p><strong>Recommended</strong></p> <ul> <li>Attend the reading of <em>Eichah</em>, if possible.</li> <li>Learn about the Beis HaMikdash and the significance of Tisha B'Av.</li> <li>Increase in tefillah, Torah study appropriate for the day, and personal reflection.</li> </ul> <p><strong>Avoid</strong></p> <ul> <li>Eating and drinking (unless medically exempt).</li> <li>Bathing or washing for pleasure.</li> <li>Wearing leather footwear.</li> <li>Applying lotions, oils, or perfumes.</li> <li>Marital relations.</li> <li>Greeting others in the usual manner.</li> <li>Sitting on regular-height chairs until <strong>chatzos</strong>, in accordance with your community's custom.</li> </ul> <h3>Children</h3> <p>Children who are not yet obligated to fast should still be encouraged to appreciate the solemnity of the day in an age-appropriate manner.</p> <p><strong>Encouraged</strong></p> <ul> <li>Read or learn about the Beis HaMikdash and Jewish history.</li> <li>Participate in quiet, meaningful family activities.</li> </ul> <p><strong>Avoid</strong></p> <ul> <li>Games, music, movies, and other forms of entertainment.</li> <li>Special treats or unnecessary indulgences, as appropriate for the child's age and family custom.</li> </ul> <p>Please follow your family's <strong>minhagim</strong> and consult your <strong>Rav</strong> with any questions regarding the observance of Tisha B'Av.</p> <p><strong>May we merit to see the rebuilding of the Beis HaMikdash במהרה בימינו, and may these days of mourning soon be transformed into days of joy.</strong></p>
