Tisha B'Av Reminder: A Guide for Adults & Children

July 22, 2026

As we observe Tisha B'Av, we mourn the destruction of the Beis HaMikdash and reflect on how we can strengthen our relationship with Hashem and with one another.

Adults

Recommended

  • Attend the reading of Eichah, if possible.
  • Learn about the Beis HaMikdash and the significance of Tisha B'Av.
  • Increase in tefillah, Torah study appropriate for the day, and personal reflection.

Avoid

  • Eating and drinking (unless medically exempt).
  • Bathing or washing for pleasure.
  • Wearing leather footwear.
  • Applying lotions, oils, or perfumes.
  • Marital relations.
  • Greeting others in the usual manner.
  • Sitting on regular-height chairs until chatzos, in accordance with your community's custom.

Children

Children who are not yet obligated to fast should still be encouraged to appreciate the solemnity of the day in an age-appropriate manner.

Encouraged

  • Read or learn about the Beis HaMikdash and Jewish history.
  • Participate in quiet, meaningful family activities.

Avoid

  • Games, music, movies, and other forms of entertainment.
  • Special treats or unnecessary indulgences, as appropriate for the child's age and family custom.

Please follow your family's minhagim and consult your Rav with any questions regarding the observance of Tisha B'Av.

May we merit to see the rebuilding of the Beis HaMikdash במהרה בימינו, and may these days of mourning soon be transformed into days of joy.
