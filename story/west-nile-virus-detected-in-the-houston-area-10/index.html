West Nile Virus Detected in the Houston Area

July 18, 2026
Mosquito season is in full swing, and Houston-area residents are encouraged to take extra precautions after West Nile virus activity was detected in local mosquito populations.

Most people infected with West Nile virus do not develop symptoms, but some may experience fever, headache, body aches, fatigue, nausea, or a skin rash. In rare cases, the virus can cause serious neurological illness, particularly in older adults and those with weakened immune systems.

To help reduce your risk:
🦟 Use EPA-approved insect repellent.
👕 Wear long sleeves and pants when outdoors at dawn and dusk.
💧 Eliminate standing water around your home where mosquitoes can breed.
🪟 Keep windows and doors properly screened.

If you develop severe symptoms after a mosquito bite, contact your healthcare provider.

Stay informed, stay protected, and help keep our community safe this mosquito season.
