The Nine Days – A Friendly Reminder

July 15, 2026

<p class="isSelectedEnd">The Nine Days (from <strong>Rosh Chodesh Av</strong> through <strong>Tisha B'Av</strong>) are a time of mourning for the destruction of the Beis HaMikdash. During these days, we minimize expressions of joy and focus on strengthening our connection to Hashem through Torah, tefillah, tzedakah, and acts of kindness.</p><p class="isSelectedEnd"><strong>During the Nine Days, many observe the following customs:</strong><br>• No meat or wine (except on Shabbos or at a seudas mitzvah)<br>• No swimming or bathing for pleasure<br>• No laundry or wearing freshly laundered clothing<br>• No home renovations or decorating<br>• No purchasing new clothing or other items that bring joy<br>• No cutting nails<br>• No planting</p><p class="isSelectedEnd"><br></p><p class="isSelectedEnd">These customs are <strong>in addition to the Three Weeks</strong>, during which we also refrain from:<br>• Weddings<br>• Haircuts and shaving<br>• Listening to live or recorded music</p><p class="isSelectedEnd">As always, customs may vary by community. Please follow your family minhag or consult your Rav with any questions.</p><p><strong>May we merit to see the rebuilding of the Beis HaMikdash במהרה בימינו.</strong></p>
